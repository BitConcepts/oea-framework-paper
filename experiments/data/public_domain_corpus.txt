No one ought to feel surprise at much remaining as yet unexplained in regard to the origin of species and varieties.
When we look to the individuals of the same variety or sub-variety of our older cultivated plants and animals, one of the first points which strikes us is that they generally differ more from each other than do the individuals of any one species in a state of nature.
The general conclusion at which I have slowly been driven is that the conditions of life do not act as the sole causes of variability.
Variability is governed by many unknown laws, more especially by that of correlation of growth.
