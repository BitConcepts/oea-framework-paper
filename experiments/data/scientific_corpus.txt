Every body continues in its state of rest or of uniform motion in a right line unless it is compelled to change that state by forces impressed upon it.
The change of motion is proportional to the motive force impressed and is made in the direction of the right line in which that force is impressed.
To every action there is always opposed an equal reaction; or, the mutual actions of two bodies upon each other are always equal and directed to contrary parts.
If a body impinge upon another, and by its force change the motion of the other, that body also will undergo an equal change in its own motion toward the contrary part.

A recursive system that generates its own training data faces a fundamental challenge: it cannot distinguish its own outputs from external ground truth.
Without an external reference signal, the system drifts toward the most probable next token, which is increasingly its own prior generation.
The entropy of a closed generative system increases over time unless constrained by information from outside the system boundary.
Calibration is the alignment between a model's expressed confidence and its actual accuracy. When calibration degrades, expressed certainty becomes decoupled from ground truth.
The epistemic agent knows the limits of its knowledge; the miscalibrated agent does not know what it does not know.
Stability in a recursive learning system requires that each generative step be anchored to evidence that was not itself generated by the system.
When the anchor is removed, the system is free to drift, and drift, once begun, compounds with each subsequent generation.
The ontological boundary defines what counts as valid output. Without it, the system has no principle for rejecting its own errors.
Retrieval-augmented generation grounds synthesis in corpus evidence, but retrieval alone is not sufficient if the retrieval quality is not itself monitored and filtered.
The full prevention of collapse requires three separable mechanisms: bounding the output space, filtering by evidence quality, and closing the feedback loop with measured outcomes.
