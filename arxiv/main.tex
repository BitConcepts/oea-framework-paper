\begin{abstract}
We present the OEA (Ontology, Epistemic, Agentic) framework as a testable, three-layer protocol
for improving reliability in agentic systems under recursive synthetic-data exposure. OEA frames
stability as an intervention hypothesis: ontological constraint definition bounds output space,
retrieval-augmented epistemic filtering grounds claims in corpus evidence and scores them by
calibration quality, and agentic closure operationalizes the feedback loop empirically.
We report two experiments: (1) a bigram-proxy ablation study across 12 variants---including a
calibration-quality-derived parameterization and a novel \emph{anti-calibrated}
falsification control---showing the full OEA protocol achieves the highest true-rejection rate
(0.890) and lowest false-rejection rate (0.090) across 648 robustness-sweep runs per variant;
(2) a real LLM validation using distilgpt2 with BM25 corpus-grounded retrieval and genuine
neural log-probability epistemic filtering. The anti-calibrated variant (inverting log-probability
selection) constitutes a causal proof-of-mechanism: if it degrades faster than the no-filter
baseline, calibration quality is the operative variable, not filtering per se.
All experimental artifacts are pre-registered and reproducible in this repository.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Model collapse has become a central concern in recursive synthetic training pipelines, with evidence of degradation under specific feedback-loop assumptions \cite{shumailov2024modelcollapse,fu2025theoretical}. At the same time, subsequent studies show collapse behavior depends strongly on data-mixing assumptions, curation policy, and accumulation dynamics rather than being a single universal outcome \cite{gerstgrasser2024inevitable,schaeffer2025position}. This motivates a controlled framework that separates what is known, what is hypothesized, and what is empirically testable.

The OEA framework is proposed as such a structure. \textbf{Ontology} defines boundary conditions of valid outputs. \textbf{Epistemic} introduces explicit falsification and calibration checks for generated claims. \textbf{Agentic} operationalizes interventions in measurable experiment loops. Philosophically, this aligns with a Popperian criterion: claims must be framed so they can fail under evidence \cite{popper1959logic}.

\section{Related Work and Evidence Positioning}
\subsection{Recursive Synthetic Training and Collapse Dynamics}
Empirical and theoretical analyses report failure modes in recursive loops, including loss of tail behavior and quality drift \cite{shumailov2024modelcollapse,fu2025theoretical}. Newer analyses also identify mitigation regimes, especially when real data are retained or accumulated \cite{gerstgrasser2024inevitable}. Therefore, we treat collapse as regime-dependent rather than monolithic.

\subsection{Grounding and Retrieval-Augmented Generation}
RAG methods improve factual grounding by coupling generation with retrievable evidence \cite{lewis2020rag}. However, retrieval does not eliminate faithfulness errors by itself; robust evaluation still requires claim-level verification and calibration-aware diagnostics.

\subsection{Calibration as Trust Signal}
Confidence reliability is not guaranteed by raw model scores. Expected Calibration Error (ECE) and related diagnostics remain practical tools for measuring confidence-quality alignment \cite{guo2017calibration}. OEA uses calibration as a monitored variable, not a proxy for truth.

\subsection{Trust-Aware Orchestration in Agentic Pipelines}
Recent trust-aware orchestration proposals provide implementation motifs for confidence-mediated arbitration and retrieval-triggered re-evaluation loops \cite{roumeliotis2025trust}. In this paper, such mechanisms are treated as design inspirations requiring domain-specific validation.

\section{Methodology}
\subsection{OEA Tri-Layer Protocol}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Ontological Anchoring}: define non-negotiable domain constraints, variable definitions, and invalid-output criteria before inference.
  \item \textbf{Epistemic Filtering}: apply retrieval-grounded falsification attempts, label evidence provenance, and compute calibration diagnostics (including ECE).
  \item \textbf{Agentic Closure}: run pre-registered interventions, capture outcomes, and update subsequent hypothesis tests only through documented evidence deltas.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Pilot Experiment Design}
\textbf{E1: Recursive Stability Test.} For each seed, run 10 recursive generations where output at step $t-1$ becomes input at step $t$. Compare control vs OEA on divergence and stability metrics.

\textbf{E2: Epistemic Friction Test.} Inject synthetic falsehoods and measure rejection/acceptance behavior. Report both false accepts and false rejects.

\textbf{Execution metadata.} We executed 30 seeded runs per condition and exported machine-readable artifacts in \texttt{results/}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{recursive\_stability\_runs.csv}
  \item \texttt{epistemic\_friction\_runs.csv}
  \item \texttt{summary\_metrics.json}
\end{itemize}

\section{Real LLM Validation}

\subsection{Design and Motivation}

To address the scope limitations of the bigram proxy harness and to establish whether the OEA
epistemic filter operates on real neural probability distributions, we designed a second experiment
using \texttt{distilgpt2} (82M parameters; RAG architecture following \cite{lewis2020rag}).
The experiment replaces simulation-layer probability constants with genuine model log-probabilities
and implements the full three-layer OEA protocol.

\textbf{Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) — Ontological Anchoring.}
A \texttt{BM25Retriever} indexes the seed corpus into passage-level units using token-overlap
similarity: $\text{score}(q, p) = |q \cap p| / \sqrt{|q| \cdot |p|}$. At each generation step,
the highest-scoring passage is prepended to the prompt, constraining synthesis within the
domain boundary of the original corpus. This is corpus-grounded retrieval, not a log-probability
proxy.

\textbf{Epistemic filtering.} $K{=}3$ candidate continuations are generated per step and scored
by their mean log-probability under the \emph{original frozen model}: $s_{\text{verify}}(y|x) =
\log G_0(y|x)$, directly implementing the verification score of \cite{fu2025selfverification}.
The highest-scoring candidate is retained (\texttt{oea\_anchored}).

\textbf{Anti-calibrated control.} \texttt{oea\_miscalibrated} keeps the \emph{lowest}-log-prob
candidate. If this variant degrades faster than even the no-RAG \texttt{control}, it constitutes
causal evidence that calibration quality is the operative variable, not filtering per se.

\textbf{Ablation: RAG-only baseline.} \texttt{oea\_rag\_only} uses retrieval without epistemic
filtering, isolating the retrieval contribution from the log-prob selection mechanism.

\textbf{Frozen-weights scope.} Model weights are not updated; this is a generation-time experiment.
We interpret this as a \emph{necessary-condition} test (DEC-004): if log-probability-based
filtering cannot reduce distributional drift in the idealized frozen setting, it cannot do so
during training. Results establish a lower bound on in-training efficacy and do not substitute
for fine-tuning experiments (acknowledged in Limitations).

\section{Discussion}
The pilot results support the directional OEA hypothesis in the implemented harness: stronger recursive stability and improved synthetic-noise rejection tradeoffs relative to control. However, this evidence should be interpreted as \emph{pilot computational validation}, not definitive proof of universal behavior across model families or domains.

\section{Limitations and Threats to Validity}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bigram proxy scope}: the ablation study uses a bigram language model as a proxy
  for recursive LLM degradation. Results are scope-bounded to this regime.
  \item \textbf{Frozen-weights scope}: the real LLM validation uses a frozen \texttt{distilgpt2};
  it measures generation-time distributional drift, not training-time weight degradation.
  We interpret this as a necessary-condition test (DEC-004), not a substitute for fine-tuning
  experiments, which remain future work.
  \item \textbf{Calibration-quality parameterization}: the bigram ablation uses a
  principled calibration-quality (CQ) formula; CQ values for the full OEA variant are derived
  from the real LLM measurement output and documented in \texttt{real\_lm\_summary.json}.
  \item \textbf{External validity}: results may not transfer to all architectures, corpora, or
  training regimes. Cross-domain validation is future work.
  \item \textbf{Retrieval precision}: evidence quality is bounded by the seed corpus coverage;
  BM25 token-overlap retrieval does not account for semantic similarity.
  \item \textbf{Metric insufficiency}: no single metric is exhaustive. ECE proxies and
  log-probability thresholds are practical but not sufficient as truth criteria.
  \item \textbf{Preprint risk}: some cited works are preprint-stage and may change after peer
  review. Citation versions are locked in \texttt{arxiv/references.bib}.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Ethics.} Confidence estimates and trust scores must not be treated as guarantees of
truth, especially in safety-critical deployments. OEA improves calibration within a
specified regime; it does not eliminate hallucination or guarantee factual correctness.

\section{Conclusion}

We presented the OEA (Ontology, Epistemic, Agentic) framework as a testable, three-layer
intervention hypothesis for improving reliability in agentic systems under recursive synthetic-data
exposure. Our pilot computational experiments support the directional hypothesis: the full OEA
pipeline achieves the highest true-rejection rate (0.890) and the lowest false-rejection rate (0.090)
across all 11 tested variants, with a stability delta of $+0.442$ ($d=3.02$, $p<0.001$) relative to
the hardest baseline (\texttt{control\_replace}).

The ablation study reveals a key asymmetry: certain single-layer ablations
(\texttt{ablation\_A}, \texttt{ablation\_EA}) achieve comparable or superior raw stability scores to
\texttt{oea\_full}, yet exhibit substantially weaker epistemic filtering. This suggests that stability
and epistemic reliability are partially orthogonal, and that the full three-layer protocol is
necessary when both properties are required jointly.

These findings must be interpreted with explicit scope bounds. The current evidence derives
entirely from a controlled bigram-proxy simulation harness and does not constitute proof of universal
behavior across production language models, retrieval corpora, or downstream task domains. The claim
is that OEA provides measurable improvement in this regime under these conditions.

Future work will focus on: (1) integrating the OEA protocol with production agentic runtimes
beyond the simulation harness; (2) validating calibration-quality alignment using real ECE
measurements on held-out LLM outputs; (3) extending the ablation design to include cross-domain
corpora and retrieval-augmented variants; and (4) developing automated tooling for epistemic
boundary enforcement in deployed agent pipelines.
