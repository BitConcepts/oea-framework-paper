\begin{abstract}
We present an empirical study of the OEA (Ontology, Epistemic, Agentic) framework as a three-layer protocol for improving reliability in agentic systems under recursive synthetic-data exposure.
The central thesis is: \emph{recursive generative stability depends more strongly on calibration direction and epistemic filtering than on unconstrained retrieval augmentation.}
``Ontology'' in this work refers to structured distributional anchoring---not philosophical ontology.
``Agentic'' refers to recursive persistence dynamics---not autonomous agency.
We report four experiments: (1) a bigram-proxy ablation study across 12 variants with calibration-quality parameterization, (2) a four-model real LLM validation on \texttt{distilgpt2} (82M), \texttt{gpt2} (124M), \texttt{EleutherAI/gpt-neo-125M} (125M), and \texttt{Qwen/Qwen2.5-1.5B} (1.5B, modern 2024 architecture) with BM25 corpus-grounded retrieval, (3) a 30-step recursive memory drift benchmark measuring entity retention and semantic drift, and (4) a baseline competition comparing OEA against temperature reduction, top-$k$, entropy filtering, and repetition penalty.
The full OEA protocol achieves the highest true-rejection rate (TRR\,=\,0.836) and lowest false-rejection rate (FRR\,=\,0.081) across 648 robustness-sweep runs per variant ($d=4.56$, $p<0.001$).
The anti-calibrated variant provides strong ablation evidence for calibration direction as the operative variable: inverting the selection signal degrades mean log-probability by $-0.55$--$-1.37$ nats, while full OEA improves it by $+0.62$--$+1.63$ nats across all four models and three architecture families.
Results are fully reproducible (\texttt{torch.manual\_seed}, CUDA 12.1) and artifacts are committed to this repository.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\paragraph{Primary thesis.}
This work argues that recursive generative stability depends more strongly on \emph{calibration direction} and \emph{epistemic filtering} than on unconstrained retrieval augmentation. Retrieval without directional calibration is associated with harm to distributional fidelity; miscalibration actively reverses discrimination performance.

Model collapse has become a central concern in recursive synthetic training pipelines, with evidence of degradation under specific feedback-loop assumptions \cite{shumailov2024modelcollapse,fu2025theoretical}. Subsequent studies show collapse behavior depends strongly on data-mixing assumptions, curation policy, and accumulation dynamics \cite{gerstgrasser2024inevitable,schaeffer2025position}. This motivates a controlled framework that separates what is known, what is hypothesized, and what is empirically testable.

The OEA framework provides such a structure. \textbf{Ontological Anchoring} defines boundary conditions on the distributional vocabulary---\emph{not} formal symbolic ontology. \textbf{Epistemic Filtering} introduces explicit falsification and calibration checks for generated claims. \textbf{Agentic Closure} operationalizes interventions in measurable recursive loops---\emph{not} autonomous agency. This aligns with a Popperian criterion: claims must be framed so they can fail under evidence \cite{popper1959logic}.

\subsection{Scope and Non-Claims}
\label{sec:nonclaims}

This work makes the following explicit \textbf{non-claims}:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item No claim of artificial general intelligence or any step toward it.
  \item No claim of formal symbolic ontology in the philosophical sense; ``Ontology'' is used in its computational sense (structured distributional anchoring).
  \item No claim of true autonomous agency; ``Agentic'' refers to recursive persistence dynamics.
  \item No claim of causal proof; results demonstrate association and ablation consistency.
  \item No claim of general intelligence improvement beyond the specific experimental regime.
\end{itemize}
All results are scope-bounded to the implemented harness. Transfer to production systems or training-time dynamics remains future work.

\section{Core Hypotheses and Falsifiability}
\label{sec:hypotheses}

\begin{description}[style=nextline]
  \item[H1 (Calibration Direction)] Correct-direction calibration (highest-log-prob selection) improves recursive distributional fidelity relative to unconstrained sampling.
  \item[H2 (Miscalibration Reversal)] Inverting the calibration signal (lowest-log-prob selection) reverses discrimination: TRR falls below control and FRR rises above control.
  \item[H3 (RAG without Filter)] Retrieval augmentation without epistemic filtering is associated with degraded distributional fidelity relative to full OEA.
  \item[H4 (Memory Drift)] OEA-controlled recursion is associated with lower semantic drift than unconstrained recursion over long chains.
\end{description}

\paragraph{What would falsify OEA?}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{H1}: \texttt{oea\_anchored} log-probability is not consistently higher than \texttt{control} across all four models.
  \item \textbf{H2}: \texttt{oea\_miscalibrated} TRR is not lower than \texttt{control} TRR, or FRR is not higher.
  \item \textbf{H3}: \texttt{oea\_rag\_only} log-probability is not lower than \texttt{oea\_anchored}.
  \item \textbf{H4}: OEA-controlled semantic drift (JSD) is not lower than uncontrolled at step 30.
  \item \textbf{Baseline}: OEA does not outperform all non-OEA controls on TRR when matched for sampling budget.
\end{itemize}

\section{Notation and Formal Definitions}
\label{sec:notation}

\noindent\textbf{Symbols.}
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Symbol & Definition \\
\midrule
$x_t$ & Token sequence at recursive step $t$ \\
$P_\theta$ & Generative model with frozen parameters $\theta$ \\
$\mathcal{A}(\cdot)$ & Ontological anchoring operator (vocabulary projection) \\
$\mathcal{E}(\cdot)$ & Epistemic filter operator (candidate selection) \\
$G_0$ & Reference frozen model (original, not updated) \\
$K$ & Number of epistemic filter candidates ($K=3$) \\
$\mathrm{TRR}$ & True rejection rate (higher = better discrimination) \\
$\mathrm{FRR}$ & False rejection rate (lower = better specificity) \\
$\mathrm{JSD}(p, q)$ & Jensen-Shannon divergence \\
$\mathcal{V}_{\mathrm{ref}}$ & Reference vocabulary (token IDs from seed corpus) \\
$\tau$ & Dynamic log-prob threshold ($= \mu_{\mathrm{in-vocab}} - 1.5\,\sigma_{\mathrm{in-vocab}}$) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent\textbf{Recursive generation:}
\begin{equation}
x_{t+1} \sim P_\theta\!\left(x \mid \mathcal{A}(x_t),\; \mathcal{E}(x_t)\right)
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{Anchoring operator} (Layer 1):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{A}(x) = \bigl\{c \in C_K : \forall\, w \in c,\; w \in \mathcal{V}_{\mathrm{ref}}\bigr\}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{Epistemic filter} (Layer 2):
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{E}(x) = \argmax_{c \in C_K}\; \log G_0(c \mid x)
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{JSD stability metric:}
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{JSD}(p \| q) = \tfrac{1}{2} D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p \| m) + \tfrac{1}{2} D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q \| m), \quad m = \tfrac{p + q}{2}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{TRR / FRR:}
\begin{align}
\mathrm{TRR} &= |\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{OOV}}|^{-1}\sum_{t \in \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{OOV}}} \mathbf{1}[\log G_0(t \mid x) < \tau] \\
\mathrm{FRR} &= |\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{ref}}|^{-1}\sum_{t \in \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{ref}}} \mathbf{1}[\log G_0(t \mid x) < \tau]
\end{align}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Recursive Synthetic Training and Collapse}

Empirical and theoretical analyses report failure modes in recursive loops \cite{shumailov2024modelcollapse,fu2025theoretical}. Mitigation regimes exist when real data are retained \cite{gerstgrasser2024inevitable}.
Drayson et al.~\cite{drayson2025detection} prevent collapse at \emph{training time} via machine-generated text detection; this operates at data-curation time with labeled annotations. Zhu et al.~\cite{zhu2025synthesize} prevent collapse via token-level corpus editing before training. Kov\'{a}\v{c} et al.~\cite{kovac2025recursive} show lexical diversity amplifies distributional shift, directly motivating OEA's anchoring. Keisha et al.~\cite{keisha2025knowledge} characterize knowledge collapse as factual accuracy eroding while surface fluency persists---the failure mode OEA's epistemic layer is designed to detect.
These approaches operate at training or data-curation time and lack OEA's three-layer generation-time protocol.

\subsection{RAG, Calibration, and Trust-Aware Orchestration}

RAG methods improve grounding \cite{lewis2020rag} but retrieval alone does not eliminate faithfulness errors. Abbasi Yadkori et al.~\cite{abbasiyadkori2024believe} provide an information-theoretic separation of epistemic from aleatoric uncertainty, grounding OEA's epistemic filter. Calibration diagnostics \cite{guo2017calibration} are used as monitored variables, not truth proxies. Trust-aware orchestration proposals provide compatible implementation motifs \cite{roumeliotis2025trust}.

\subsection{Position Against Related Frameworks}

OEA differs from constitutional AI (output-time rule constraints), self-refinement (critic-model iteration), self-consistency (ensemble voting), and recursive self-improvement (weight updates) by operating as a \emph{generation-time, frozen-weights, calibration-directed} protocol. Unlike entropy regularization or repetition penalties, OEA applies directional calibration quality rather than generic distribution tightening. Section~\ref{sec:baseline} directly tests this distinction.

\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

\subsection{OEA Tri-Layer Protocol}

\noindent\textbf{Agentic loop model.} Each generation step corresponds to one agent action: the output at step $t{-}1$ conditions step $t$, forming a feedback loop over a shared context window. Our recursive loop directly models this pattern. Multi-agent coordination, tool use, and external memory are explicitly out of scope.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Ontological Anchoring}: domain constraints and invalid-output criteria. BM25 retrieval + vocabulary projection.
  \item \textbf{Epistemic Filtering}: retrieval-grounded falsification and calibration scoring. $K{=}3$ candidate scoring under the frozen reference model.
  \item \textbf{Agentic Closure}: pre-registered interventions with documented evidence deltas.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Pilot Experiment Design}

\textbf{E1 (Recursive Stability).} 10 recursive generations per seed. Compare control vs OEA on JSD and stability.
\textbf{E2 (Epistemic Friction).} Inject synthetic falsehoods; measure TRR and FRR.
\textbf{Execution:} 30 seeded runs per condition; artifacts in \texttt{results/}.

\subsection{Ablation Study}
\label{sec:ablation}

\paragraph{Ablation interpretation.}
\textbf{Miscalibration reversal}: the CQ formula parameterizes the suite such that CQ$<$0.5 flips rejection direction: FRR rises to 0.651, TRR falls to 0.257---consistent with H2.
\textbf{ROUGE-L decline under anchoring}: vocabulary anchoring concentrates token identity to the reference domain (improving log-probability) but does not preserve original phrase sequences (lowering ROUGE-L). These metrics are orthogonal.
\textbf{JSD rise under anchoring}: anchoring concentrates output over a sub-vocabulary; the resulting distribution is more concentrated than the seed. This is expected behavior, not a failure mode.
\textbf{RAG-only degradation}: BM25 context injection without quality-aware candidate selection introduces retrieval noise. Without the epistemic filter, RAG alone yields lower log-probability than full OEA, consistent with H3.

\section{Real LLM Validation}
\label{sec:real-lm}

\subsection{Design and Motivation}

Four models spanning three architecture families: \texttt{distilgpt2} (82M) and \texttt{gpt2} (124M) from the GPT-2 family, \texttt{EleutherAI/gpt-neo-125M} (125M) from the GPT-Neo family (local attention), and \texttt{Qwen/Qwen2.5-1.5B} (1.5B) from the Qwen family (RoPE, GQA, SwiGLU; released September 2024). The Qwen model is 10$\times$ larger than the other models and uses a modern architecture with grouped-query attention and rotary position embeddings, directly addressing the reviewer concern that results may be small-model or legacy-architecture artifacts. The harness uses \texttt{AutoModelForCausalLM}/\texttt{AutoTokenizer} for model-agnostic loading.

\textbf{BM25 Retrieval (Layer 1).} $\text{score}(q, p) = |q \cap p| / \sqrt{|q| \cdot |p|}$. Retrieved passage prepended.

\textbf{Epistemic filtering (Layer 2).} $K{=}3$ candidates scored by $\log G_0(y|x)$ \cite{fu2025selfverification}.

\textbf{Frozen-weights scope (DEC-004).} This is a necessary-condition test: results establish a lower bound on in-training efficacy.

\paragraph{H1 (Calibration Direction, supported).}
\texttt{oea\_anchored} improves log-probability by $+0.62$ nats (distilgpt2), $+1.63$ nats (gpt2), $+0.82$ nats (gpt-neo-125M), and $+0.88$ nats (Qwen2.5-1.5B). The effect is consistent across all four models and three architecture families, including a modern 1.5B-parameter model.

\paragraph{H2 (Miscalibration Reversal, supported).}
\texttt{oea\_miscalibrated} degrades log-probability below the no-RAG control ($-1.14$, $-0.55$, $-1.37$, and $-0.62$ nats across the four models), providing ablation evidence consistent with H2 \cite{fu2025selfverification,abbasiyadkori2024believe}.

\texttt{oea\_anchored} achieves lower ROUGE-L than control; \texttt{oea\_miscalibrated} achieves higher ROUGE-L. The systematic dissociation confirms log-probability selection and ROUGE-L evaluation are orthogonal, ruling out metric circularity. See Figure~\ref{fig:dissociation}.

\paragraph{H3 (RAG without Filter, supported).}
\texttt{oea\_rag\_only} degrades log-probability ($-0.54$, $-0.08$, $-0.43$, $-0.07$ nats), consistent with H3: the epistemic filter, not retrieval per se, is the operative component.

\section{Agentic Memory Drift Benchmark}
\label{sec:memorydrift}

A 30-step recursive memory drift benchmark (\texttt{experiments/recursive\_memory\_drift.py}) compares \textbf{oea\_controlled} vs \textbf{uncontrolled} over 20 seeds in the bigram proxy harness.

\textbf{Metrics.} Entity retention (Jaccard overlap with initial token set); semantic drift (JSD from initial distribution); vocabulary collapse (unique-token ratio).

\textbf{Results.} At step 30: OEA-controlled semantic drift JSD $-0.006$ lower (95\% CI overlapping); entity retention $\Delta = -0.001$ (negligible). Hallucination proxy is zero for both variants---a limitation of the bigram harness, which cannot generate out-of-vocabulary tokens by construction.

\textbf{Interpretation.} The bigram harness provides only structural support for H4. Effect sizes ($|\Delta| < 0.01$) reflect the vocabulary constraints inherent to the bigram model. Neural model validation of memory drift is necessary for quantitatively meaningful conclusions and remains future work. Source: \texttt{results/memory\_drift/}.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

Results are consistent with the primary thesis across four complementary experiments. The four-model, three-architecture validation confirms the mechanism via log-probability improvement and ROUGE-L dissociation---two orthogonal metrics. Cross-architecture consistency (GPT-2, GPT-Neo, and Qwen2.5) and cross-scale consistency (82M to 1.5B) strengthen external validity. This evidence should be interpreted as pilot computational validation, not proof of universal behavior.

The key asymmetry: stability and epistemic reliability are partially orthogonal. Single-layer ablations can achieve comparable stability while exhibiting substantially weaker epistemic filtering. The full three-layer protocol is necessary when both properties are jointly required.

The baseline competition supports the claim that OEA is not reducible to generic constraint tightening: OEA achieves higher TRR than all five non-OEA controls. However, this finding is not statistically significant at $N{=}20$ seeds and requires real LLM validation for conclusive evidence.

\section{Implications for Recursive Agent Systems}
\label{sec:implications}

The substrate-level mechanisms validated here---directional calibration and epistemic filtering under recursive synthetic exposure---are relevant to several failure modes observed in recursive agent architectures. We note these connections cautiously, as transfer from our frozen-weights harness to production systems remains unvalidated.

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Recursive planning drift}: agents that iteratively refine plans accumulate distributional shift at each step; calibration-directed filtering may reduce plan degradation.
  \item \textbf{Hallucinated refinement}: self-correcting systems can amplify confident errors when the correction signal is miscalibrated; the anti-calibrated condition (H2) directly models this failure.
  \item \textbf{Synthetic contamination in memory}: persistent memory systems that store and retrieve model-generated content face progressive contamination; ontological anchoring provides a mechanism for domain-boundary enforcement.
  \item \textbf{Specification degradation}: recursive specification refinement (e.g., iterative requirements generation) is vulnerable to semantic drift and contradiction accumulation.
\end{itemize}

These connections are \emph{suggestive, not demonstrated}. The experiments in this paper establish substrate-level mechanisms; whether they transfer to full agent architectures with tool use, multi-step planning, and external memory is the subject of future work. We explicitly do not claim improvements to autonomous reasoning, planning competence, or general intelligence.

\section{Failure Modes and Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Bigram proxy scope}: cannot model long-range dependencies, contextual disambiguation, or neural hallucination. Results scope-bounded to this regime.
  \item \textbf{Frozen-weights scope}: not a substitute for fine-tuning experiments.
  \item \textbf{Over-anchoring harms diversity}: vocabulary anchoring improves log-probability but reduces ROUGE-L recall and can impoverish creative or cross-domain generation.
  \item \textbf{Hallucination proxy limitation}: the bigram harness hallucination proxy (OOV rate) is always zero; neural model validation required.
  \item \textbf{Domain mismatch}: anchoring will degrade for out-of-domain inputs; BM25 does not handle semantic similarity.
  \item \textbf{Calibration-quality mismatch (UNK-001)}: the dynamic-threshold TRR metric does not directly validate bigram-suite CQ (measured CQ\,=\,0.446 vs design estimate 0.83). Direct ECE validation is future work.
  \item \textbf{Scale limitations}: results validated on models from 82M to 1.5B parameters across three architecture families; extrapolation to frontier-scale models ($>$10B) is not warranted.
  \item \textbf{Baseline comparison power}: $N{=}20$ seeds insufficient for statistically significant pairwise comparisons in baseline competition.
  \item \textbf{Causal language}: all causal language is provisional. We use ``consistent with,'' ``associated with,'' and ``provides ablation evidence for'' throughout.
  \item \textbf{Preprint citations}: some cited works are preprints and may change after peer review.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented OEA as a testable, three-layer intervention hypothesis for improving reliability in recursive agentic systems, with explicit non-claims: no AGI, no formal symbolic ontology, no autonomous agency, no causal proof, no general intelligence improvement.

Our experiments support the primary thesis across four complementary conditions. The full OEA protocol achieves TRR\,=\,0.836, FRR\,=\,0.081 ($d=4.56$, $p<0.001$) and improves mean log-probability by $+0.62$--$+1.63$ nats across four models and three architecture families (82M--1.5B parameters). The anti-calibrated control provides strong ablation evidence for calibration direction as the operative variable. Metric dissociation confirms log-probability and ROUGE-L capture orthogonal quality dimensions.

These findings carry explicit scope bounds and must not be extrapolated to production systems without further evidence.

Future work: (1) frontier-scale model validation ($>$10B parameters); (2) production agentic runtime integration with tool use and persistent memory; (3) ECE-based CQ validation; (4) neural memory drift experiments; (5) statistically powered real LLM baseline comparison.

\section{Statistical Methods}
\label{app:stats}

\paragraph{Permutation test.} Two-sided, $n_{\mathrm{perm}}=2000$ shuffles. Test statistic: absolute mean difference. Reported as $p < 0.001$ when $({\mathrm{count}+1})/(n_{\mathrm{perm}}+1) < 0.001$.

\paragraph{Confidence intervals.} $t$-based 95\% CIs: $\hat{\mu} \pm t_{0.025, n-1} \cdot \hat{\sigma}/\sqrt{n}$. Bigram suite: $n=12$ seeds. Real LLM: $n=10$ seeds ($t_{0.025,9}=2.262$). Memory drift and baseline: $n=20$ seeds ($t_{0.025,19}=2.093$).

\paragraph{Seed policy.}
Bigram suite: \texttt{numpy.random.default\_rng(seed)} per variant/seed combination.
Real LLM: \texttt{torch.manual\_seed(gen\_seed)}, $\texttt{gen\_seed} = \texttt{seed\_idx} \times 1000 + \texttt{iteration} \times 10$.
Memory drift: \texttt{random.Random(seed\_idx * 1000 + 42)}.
Baseline competition: \texttt{numpy.random.default\_rng(seed\_idx * 17 + 3)}.

\paragraph{Effect size.} Cohen's $d = (\bar{x}_A - \bar{x}_B) / s_{\mathrm{pooled}}$, $s_{\mathrm{pooled}} = \sqrt{(s_A^2 + s_B^2)/2}$.

\paragraph{Sample size rationale.} $N=12$ seeds achieves power $>0.99$ at $\alpha=0.05$ for the observed effect size ($d=4.56$). For real LLM, $N=10$ seeds with $\sigma \approx 0.3$ nats yields CIs of width $\pm 0.21$ nats, sufficient to detect the observed $+0.62$--$+1.63$ nat effects.

\paragraph{Multiple comparisons.} The primary hypothesis (\texttt{oea\_full} vs \texttt{control\_replace}) was pre-specified. Exploratory pairwise comparisons are reported without correction; interpret with caution.

\paragraph{Compute budget.} distilgpt2 (82M): $\approx 0.3$ GPU-hours. gpt2 (124M): $\approx 0.4$ GPU-hours. gpt-neo-125M (125M): $\approx 0.15$ GPU-hours. Qwen2.5-1.5B (1.5B): $\approx 0.45$ GPU-hours (RTX 4070 SUPER, CUDA 12.1). All bigram experiments: CPU only.

\section{Glossary}
\label{app:glossary}

\begin{description}[style=nextline,leftmargin=3cm]
  \item[Stability] Degree to which recursive generation preserves the initial seed's distributional properties; measured by JSD (lower = more stable).
  \item[Fidelity] Consistency of generated text with the original model's probability distribution; measured by mean log-probability (higher = better).
  \item[Anchoring] Constraining generated tokens to a reference vocabulary $\mathcal{V}_{\mathrm{ref}}$. \emph{Not} philosophical ontology.
  \item[Calibration] Alignment between model confidence (log-probability) and output quality. \emph{Calibration direction} determines whether the filter selects high-quality (correct) or low-quality (anti-calibrated) outputs.
  \item[Calibration Quality (CQ)] Scalar $\in [0,1]$: 0.5 = random; $>$0.5 = correct direction; $<$0.5 = anti-calibrated.
  \item[Recursive Exposure] Using model-generated outputs as inputs for subsequent generation steps.
  \item[Synthetic Contamination] Accumulation of model-generated (vs human-authored) content in the context window over recursive iterations.
  \item[Epistemic Filtering] Selecting candidate outputs by calibration quality (argmax over reference log-probability). Not ensemble voting or rule-based filtering.
  \item[Recursive Drift] Gradual divergence of the generated token distribution from the initial seed distribution; measured by JSD.
  \item[TRR / FRR] True/False Rejection Rate. TRR: fraction of OOV tokens correctly flagged (higher = better). FRR: fraction of in-vocabulary tokens incorrectly flagged (lower = better).
\end{description}
